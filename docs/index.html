Abstract

What does it actually cost to move frontier-scale inference away from CUDA?

We report two production-oriented deployments on a 16-device Huawei Ascend 910 system using CANN and vLLM-Ascend: a W8A8 Mixture-of-Experts judge serving tens of thousands of prompts per evaluated model, and a ~540 GB multimodal medical vision-language model evaluated on MMMU and MMMU-Pro.

Reaching a serviceable state required twelve plugin patches, deliberate disabling of throughput features to preserve numerical correctness, and external recovery scaffolding for recurring low-level faults. The result is a concrete, vendor-agnostic reference for teams evaluating alternative accelerators.

What we found

The portability tax appears at the intersections.

01

Coverage gaps compound

Multimodal input, MoE routing, sparse attention, and pipeline parallelism interacted outside the plugin’s tested envelope.

02

Correctness costs throughput

Sequence parallelism and fused MC2 were disabled; eager execution was chosen where graph capture threatened stability.

03

Device faults become operations

Opaque aicore and vector-core failures required watchdogs, health checks, log scraping, and automatic restart.

04

Scaling is non-monotonic

Aggregate throughput peaked near four streams, then fell as the waiting queue and failure rate grew.

Selected evidence

Correctness was achievable.
Efficiency and reliability were conditional.
